About me

A translator, before a designer.

I studied Translation and Interpretation before moving into design — which is probably why I still treat every product like a language someone has to learn quickly. Translating between two spoken languages and translating complex technical logic into a human interface aren't so different: both require understanding who's on the receiving end before you choose your words.

Eight years later, I'm still doing that — across cloud platforms, data tools, food-tech, and F&B operations apps.

"When I have free time, I often come up with ideas for 'if I'm unemployed in the future' and explore niche hobbies. I am a mother to a little son, a continuous runner against the waves of change, and a designer who trusts data over assumptions."

How I work

The values I don't compromise on.

🔎

Multiple perspectives, on purpose

I assume my first read of a problem is incomplete. Before committing to a direction, I look at it from the user's, the business's, and the engineer's seat.

📊

Data over opinion

I enjoy researching how people actually behave, not how we assume they behave. When a decision is contested, I look for evidence before I look for consensus.

🧩

Systems, not screens

A good screen on a broken system is a short-term fix. I'd rather spend a week on the sitemap and the design system than a day on a single flow.

🌱

Cautious, then curious

I'm careful before I'm fast. I'd rather ask one more question than ship one wrong assumption. But once the ground is solid, I keep moving.
